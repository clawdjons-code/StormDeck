This artifact is the source-of-truth rolling spec for StormDeck’s near-term build: start with a synthetic 3D reflectivity MWE in a game engine, preserve the data contract needed for archived radar, then graduate to ATD/NEXRAD ingest and eventually live PAR.

Biggest current bottleneck

Bottleneck: no executable renderer/data-contract MWE exists yet. Until a game engine can consume a simple time-indexed 3D reflectivity volume and expose slices/timeline/metadata, real radar ingest work risks optimizing for the wrong representation.

Do not do next

Do not start by parsing every radar format, implementing live PAR streaming, or making cinematic weather effects. That path maximizes complexity before proving the front-end contract. Very artistic. Also operationally useless.

Do next

Build a hard-coded game-engine desktop app that renders synthetic reflectivity frames from a stable JSON + binary/texture schema, with timeline scrub, slices, color map, storm motion arrow, and timestamp/source labels.

Product vision

StormDeck is a game-engine-powered 4D severe-weather cockpit that converts radar volumes and radar-derived objects into an interactive, geospatially aligned storm workspace. The product should help forecasters, researchers, trainers, and emergency managers answer: what changed, where, why it matters, who is affected, and what should happen next.

v0 vision: a desktop app renders a time-scrubbable synthetic supercell-like reflectivity volume with horizontal/vertical slicing, storm motion context, source/timestamp metadata, and a data interface that can later accept archived radar.

MVP architecture

1. Synthetic volume generator

Creates 30–120 time frames of plausible dBZ values: storm core, anvil/precip shield, optional hook-ish appendage, and motion/intensification trend.

2. Render cache

Stores compact 3D texture frames with metadata: grid size, spacing, origin, field, units, timestamp, source, and observed/synthetic flag.

3. Game-engine client

Loads frames, renders volume/slices, provides timeline scrubber, metadata HUD, storm motion arrow, and export screenshot/video function.

MWE acceptance criteria

Dream architecture

Live PAR/NEXRAD ingest flows through radar-edge preprocessing/QC, a central storm-state server, semantic object tracking, and multi-user game-engine clients. Emergency managers get simplified impact views; trainers get replay/scenario controls; researchers get full field/QC/sweep metadata. Future adaptive PAR tasking remains possible through a request layer, but not required for v1.

Visualization grammar

LayerVisual ruleTruth rule
ReflectivityConventional dBZ ramp; opacity by intensity/QC; avoid over-smoothing storm structure.Label synthetic, observed, interpolated, or derived.
VelocityDiverging inbound/outbound palette; optional storm-relative mode later.Show Nyquist/aliasing context; never imply tornado detection by color alone.
RotationOutline/halo on top of velocity, not opaque blob.Derived feature with confidence and source fields.
HailProbabilistic texture or contour over reflectivity/dual-pol.Derived; must show algorithm/proxy and confidence.
DebrisDistinct stipple/contour tied to dual-pol evidence.High consequence; label uncertainty aggressively.
Warning/impactCorridor polygons, towns/assets, ETA labels.Decision overlay, not radar observation.
UncertaintyHatching, transparency, or confidence badges.Uncertainty must be visible in screenshots/video exports.

UX/navigation model

ControlMWE behaviorOperational reason
Orbit/pan/zoomInspect synthetic storm volume from any angle.Spatial comprehension without implying the 3D view is automatically superior.
Timeline scrubberMove through frames; show current and lookback time.PAR value is evolution speed; the UI must make change obvious.
Horizontal sliceAltitude slider clips/planes through volume.Forecasters need familiar plan-view reasoning.
Vertical sliceUser drags cross-section line; view side profile.Reveals storm depth/tilt without visual guesswork.
What changed panelLists top synthetic deltas over 60 seconds.Reduces cognitive load under warning pressure.
Metadata HUDAlways show source, time, units, grid, status.Trust requires provenance in the operator’s visual field.
